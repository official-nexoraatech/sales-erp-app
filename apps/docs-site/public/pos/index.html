Client documentation

POS Overview

This is a pilot covering the POS module only, built entirely from reading apps/pos-frontend and the services it calls — nothing here is guessed. It's the template for documenting the rest of the ERP; once approved, the same structure and rigor extend to every other module as apps/docs-site.

The POS Terminal is a separate, dedicated app for counter billing — distinct from the main ERP web app and built to keep working with no internet connection. It's meant for cashiers ringing up walk-in and regular customers quickly: barcode/camera scan, a running cart, discounts, split payments, and a receipt, all from one screen.

Who uses it

Cashiers, and anyone with the POS_MANAGE permission (the default Cashier role has it, along with Sales Manager, Admin, and Owner roles). A manager-level role additionally has authority to approve discounts above the standard cashier limit and other overrides — see Access & Roles.

Opening the terminal

  1. Sign in

    Go to your POS URL and log in with your cashier credentials.
    Note

    Accounts with two-factor authentication enabled cannot sign in here — POS has no 2FA challenge screen. Sign in to the main ERP app first if your account requires it.

  2. Pick your branch

    You'll only see branches your account is scoped to. If your account isn't scoped to any specific branch, every branch appears — this is expected for multi-branch roles. If exactly one branch and one warehouse apply to you, the terminal skips this screen entirely.

  3. Open your shift

    Enter your opening cash amount to start a session. You can't ring up sales without an open shift.

  4. Start billing

    The terminal opens directly on the sale screen, cursor ready in the barcode field.

What's on screen

Left side: item search/scan and a quick-item grid of your fastest-moving products. Right side: the current sale — cart lines, discounts, and the Hold/Charge buttons (which become a payment panel once you tap Charge). The top bar always shows Held Sales, a link to Returns/Exchange (opens the main ERP app), End Shift, and the sync status indicator.

Continue to Billing a Sale for the full walkthrough, or jump straight to Working Offline if your store has patchy internet.

Billing a Sale

Step 1 — Add items

  • Scan a barcode with a USB scanner — the item adds instantly, with a green flash + beep for success or a red flash + error tone if the barcode isn't recognized.
  • No scanner on hand? Use the camera-scan toggle in the search box to scan with a tablet/webcam instead.
  • Or type an item name/SKU and press Enter.
  • Fast-moving items also sit as tap-to-add tiles in the quick-item grid below the search box.
How matching works

The terminal first checks its own small list of top sellers, then falls back to a full catalog barcode lookup. If neither matches, you'll see "No item found" — the barcode genuinely isn't in the system yet and needs to be added on the Items screen.

Step 2 — Adjust quantity

Use the −/+ buttons or type a quantity directly. Fractional quantities are supported (e.g. 2.5 for 2.5 metres of fabric) — useful for items sold by length rather than piece.

Step 3 — Apply a discount (if allowed)

Type a discount % on any line, or use the order-level discount to apply the same percentage to every line at once.

Cashier limit

A standard cashier can discount up to 10% per line. Anything higher is rejected — a manager (Sales Manager, Admin, or Owner) needs to log in and complete the sale.

Step 4 — Select a customer (optional)

  • Search by name or phone — matches both your locally cached customers and, when online, a live search.
  • New customer: tap + New, enter a name and phone number.
  • Leave it blank for a walk-in — the sale bills without a named customer.
  • If loyalty points are enabled for your store and the selected customer has a balance, a redeem option appears at payment.

Step 5 — Take payment

Tap Charge (or press F8) to open the payment panel.

  • Cash — enter the amount tendered; change is calculated for you.
  • Card — records the payment; card authorization itself happens on your card terminal, not in this screen.
  • UPI — shows a scannable QR code for the customer's UPI app. This is a display convenience, not a live payment-gateway check — confirm the payment came through before completing the sale.
  • Split across modes — enter an amount against more than one mode; the total must match the amount due exactly (small rounding is tolerated).
  • Redeem loyalty points — reduces the amount due before you choose a payment mode, where enabled.

Every sale from POS is paid in full at the time of sale — there's no "invoice now, pay later" option from this screen (use a manual Invoice for that; see the training guide's Invoice module).

Step 6 — Receipt

The receipt appears automatically. Choose a paper size (58mm / 80mm / A4) and:

  • Print — always available, opens your browser's print dialog.
  • Print to receipt printer — appears only on devices paired with a supported USB/serial thermal printer; also opens the cash drawer if one is wired to the printer.
  • WhatsApp / Email — only available once the sale has actually synced to the server and a customer was selected. A sale completed offline shows "Saved offline — will sync when back online" and these buttons are hidden until it syncs.

Held Sales

If you need to step away mid-sale — a customer forgot their wallet, or wants to keep browsing — tap Hold next to Charge. The cart is parked and the screen clears for the next customer.

  • Tap Held Sales in the top bar to see everything currently parked.
  • Resume restores the cart exactly as it was.
  • Discard removes it permanently.
No auto-expiry

A held sale stays in the list indefinitely — there's no automatic timeout or end-of-day cleanup. If your store holds many sales, get in the habit of discarding ones that are no longer needed so the list stays manageable.

Held on this device

A held sale lives on the terminal it was parked on — it won't show up if you resume on a different till.

Working Offline

The terminal keeps working with no internet. You can still scan items from the last-synced catalog, search and create customers, and complete sales — everything queues locally and syncs automatically once the connection returns. Nothing you enter is lost.

The sync status indicator

Shown in the top bar, it reflects how many sales/customers are still waiting to reach the server:

StateMeaning
Online, nothing pendingFully synced. Nothing to do.
Online, N pendingConnected, and N sale(s)/customer(s) are still being sent. A manual "Sync now" action is available.
OfflineNo connection right now. Everything you do is queuing locally.

When sync gets stuck

An item that fails to sync repeatedly (5 attempts) is flagged for attention with a manual retry option — usually a sign of a persistent connection problem. If it keeps recurring, tell your manager; there may be a server-side issue worth investigating.

Stock conflicts

A queued sale can fail to sync for a different reason: the stock for an item changed while you were offline (e.g. another till sold the last units). This is handled explicitly, not just retried:

While offline
Sale queued locally with the quantity you entered
→
On sync
Server finds insufficient stock for a line
→
You choose
Adjust quantity to what's available, or cancel the sale
→
Outcome
Exactly one result: adjusted invoice, or nothing charged
Simplified flow — see the Developer track's Offline Sync Design for the underlying mechanism.

Either way you'll never end up with a duplicate invoice or a silently lost sale — the system guarantees one outcome per queued sale, even if you're forced to retry several times.

Session expired while offline?

If you're pushed back to the login screen after being offline a long time, your session simply expired — log in again. Anything already queued is still saved locally and will sync once you're logged in and back online.

Shift Management

A shift ("session") groups all sales rung up between opening and closing the till, and reconciles cash at the end.

  1. Open shift

    Enter your opening cash float before you can start billing.

  2. Bill as normal

    Every sale during the shift is tallied against it.

  3. Close shift

    The summary screen shows expected cash based on sales recorded; enter your actual counted cash and the system shows the variance.

You must have an open shift to reach the sale screen at all — the terminal redirects you to open one first if none is active.

Super Admin documentation

Access & Roles

POS authorization is coarse by design: almost every POS action is gated by a single permission, with two narrower overrides layered on top.

The core permission

POS_MANAGE gates essentially everything in the terminal — opening/closing shifts, ringing sales, held sales, quick items, customer search, UPI display, and sending digital receipts. There's no finer split (e.g. no separate "can close shift" vs. "can open shift").

Default role grants

RoleRelevant permissions
Cashier (default POS role)POS_MANAGE INVOICE_CREATE CUSTOMER_CREATE PAYMENT_CREATE SALE_RETURN_CREATE ITEM_VIEW STOCK_VIEW — does not include DISCOUNT_OVERRIDE, PRICE_LIST_VIEW, or WAREHOUSE_VIEW
Sales Manager and aboveEverything Cashier has, plus DISCOUNT_OVERRIDE, PRICE_OVERRIDE, CREDIT_LIMIT_OVERRIDE, PRICE_FLOOR_OVERRIDE

The 10% cashier discount cap

A hardcoded server-side limit — a Cashier-role user cannot apply more than 10% discount on any line, regardless of any store policy configured elsewhere. Only a user whose role grants DISCOUNT_OVERRIDE can exceed it. This is enforced on the server, so it can't be bypassed from the terminal itself.

Manager overrides have no POS screen

Sales Manager and above are granted PRICE_OVERRIDE, PRICE_FLOOR_OVERRIDE, and CREDIT_LIMIT_OVERRIDE in the role system, but the POS terminal itself has no UI that exercises any of them — a price floor violation always hard-blocks a POS sale, even for a manager, because the terminal never sends the override flag. If your business process depends on managers overriding a price floor or credit limit at the counter, that currently has to happen through the main ERP app's manual invoice screen, not POS.

Branch isolation

Users are scoped to specific branches via their account. Opening a shift and completing a sale are both checked against this scope on the server — a user cannot open a shift or bill for a branch they aren't assigned to.

Empty branch scope means "all branches"

If a user's account has no branches explicitly assigned, both the branch picker and the server-side check treat that as unrestricted — every branch is shown and billing is allowed anywhere. Confirm this is the intended behavior for your rollout (e.g. reserved for owner/admin-level accounts) rather than an oversight, since it's easy to create a normal staff account that unintentionally ends up with this "no restriction" scope if branch assignment is skipped during setup.

A few POS endpoints skip branch checks

Held sales, quick items, customer search, UPI QR, and receipt sending are scoped to the tenant but not to the specific branch. In practice this only matters for a user who somehow references another branch's session or held-sale by ID, since the sale and shift endpoints themselves are branch-checked — but it's worth knowing if you're auditing data isolation between branches.

Feature Flags

Loyalty points

Loyalty earn/redeem on POS sales is gated by a single tenant-level feature flag. When it's off, points simply aren't earned or redeemed — no error, no UI, it silently does nothing.

SettingValue
Earn rate₹100 spent = 1 point
Redeem rate1 point = ₹0.50
Rates are hardcoded, not per-tenant

Both rates above are fixed in the codebase, not editable per tenant. If different clients need different loyalty economics, that's a development change today, not a settings change.

Branch & Warehouse Setup

Before staff can use POS at a location, that location needs a branch and at least one warehouse set up in the main ERP app, and staff accounts need to be scoped to the correct branch(es).

  • A Cashier-role account does not have permission to view the warehouse list independently — the terminal handles this expected 403 gracefully with a manual warehouse-ID fallback, but it's worth knowing when troubleshooting a cashier's "can't find my warehouse" report.
  • If exactly one branch and one warehouse apply to a user, the branch-selection screen is skipped entirely for them — useful for single-location tenants.
Multi-state GST tenants — read before rollout

Every POS sale is currently computed as if the selling branch is in Maharashtra (state code 27), regardless of where the branch actually is. For a tenant whose branches are all in Maharashtra this is invisible. For any tenant with a branch outside Maharashtra, POS sales from that branch will be taxed as intra-state (CGST+SGST) when they should be inter-state (IGST) — a real GST compliance issue, not just a display quirk. Do not enable POS for out-of-state branches until this is fixed in the application.

Super Admin documentation

Limitations to Know

A rollup of things worth knowing before depending on POS operationally. Full technical detail is on the Known Issues page.

  • GST state code is hardcoded to Maharashtra (27) for every tenant — see previous page. Highest-priority item for any multi-state client.
  • Held sales never expire — they accumulate until someone manually resumes or discards them.
  • Invoice numbers are timestamp-based (POS-{tenant}-{millisecond timestamp}), not a sequential counter. Confirm with your accountant/auditor whether this satisfies your jurisdiction's sequential-invoice-numbering expectations for GST.
  • Two-factor accounts can't sign in to POS — only the main ERP app supports MFA login today.
  • UPI, card, and cheque payments are all trust-based from POS — there is no live gateway/bank verification; the cashier is confirming payment was received, same as cash.

Guards are trust-based, not re-validated

All three guards are client-side only (token presence, a persisted branch, a server-confirmed session) — POSScreen itself reads sessionId/branch with a non-null assertion, trusting the guard chain already ran. There is no client-side permission (RBAC) check anywhere in pos-frontend — authorization is enforced entirely by each backend endpoint's requirePermission(), which returns 403 if a caller lacks it. This is a deliberate architecture, not a gap: the UI simply doesn't attempt to hide actions a user can't perform.

Offline Sync Design

Client storage

Dexie database pos-offline (4 schema versions), tables: pendingSales, pendingCustomers, catalogItems, customers (cache), priceListItems, taxRates, heldSales, syncMeta, authTokens.

apps/pos-frontend/src/db.ts:125-179

Two unrelated "outbox" concepts — don't conflate them

This client-local Dexie queue is entirely separate from the server-side outboxEvents table used by InvoiceService for cross-service events (INVOICE_CREATED etc., consumed by search-service/gst-service/accounting-service). Same word, two different mechanisms.

Idempotency — confirmed end-to-end

1
Client generates operationId (UUID) at queue time, reused on every retry
→
2
Sent as operationId in the sale/customer payload
→
3
Stored as client_operation_id, backed by a unique DB constraint
→
4
Retry hits the constraint → server polls for the original result and returns it (200) instead of duplicating
Production docs site: render this as a real Mermaid sequence diagram against pos.routes.ts / InvoiceService.ts.

Applies identically to sale creation (invoices.client_operation_id, migration 0031_offline02_pos_sale_idempotency.sql) and customer creation (customers.client_operation_id, migration 0032_offline05_customer_idempotency.sql). If the poll for the original result never resolves, the client gets a clean 409 DUPLICATE_OPERATION_PROCESSING rather than a silent failure.

Verified by apps/sales-service/src/__tests__/offline02-pos-sale-idempotency.test.ts and apps/pos-frontend/src/__tests__/crossCutting.test.tsx:35-77 (exactly-once sync across a token refresh mid-batch).

Sync triggers

No single sync loop — four independent triggers, all converging on the same queue:

  1. window online event → sync pending customers, then sales, then reference data
  2. Manual "Sync now" button (shown only when online with pending items)
  3. Chromium/Android Background Sync API (tag sync-pending-sales) — not supported on Safari/iOS/Firefox
  4. Service worker posts BACKGROUND_SYNC_DONE back to open tabs to refresh counters

apps/pos-frontend/src/POSScreen.tsx:293-330

Retry & stuck handling

MAX_RETRIES = 5 — after 5 failed attempts, an item flips to status: 'stuck' and stops auto-retrying until a manual reset. Stock conflicts are a distinct sub-case, not counted toward this limit: an INSUFFICIENT_STOCK response immediately marks the item stuck with a conflict payload and routes to StockConflictModal rather than the generic retry path. Resolving via "adjust & retry" re-queues under a new operationId, since the original invoice was already voided server-side.

apps/pos-frontend/src/offlineDb.ts:8,49-51; apps/sales-service/src/api/pos.routes.ts:298-301

Stock deduction mechanism

No reservation table is used. Stock is deducted atomically at confirm time via a conditional update — UPDATE items SET available_qty = available_qty - qty WHERE available_qty >= qty RETURNING — inside the same transaction that confirms the invoice. Zero rows affected ⇒ InsufficientStockError. This correctly resolves two concurrent tills racing for the last unit: whichever transaction's update commits first wins.

apps/sales-service/src/domain/InvoiceService.ts:395-420

Known Issues & Needs Review

Everything below was found while reading the actual POS code for this pilot, not inferred. Ranked roughly by real-world impact.

GST state code hardcoded to Maharashtra (27)

placeOfSupply and sellerStateCode are hardcoded to '27' in POSScreen.tsx:519-520, regardless of the branch's actual location. Any non-Maharashtra branch gets incorrect CGST/SGST vs. IGST splitting on every POS sale. Compliance risk, not cosmetic.

Invoice numbering is timestamp-based, not sequential

POS-{tenantId}-{Date.now()} — no gapless counter. Needs a business/compliance decision on whether this satisfies sequential-invoice-numbering rules for GST audit purposes. pos.routes.ts:245

Empty branch scope = unrestricted access

A JWT with no branchIds is treated as "all branches" on both client and server. Confirm this is intentional (e.g. reserved for owner/admin accounts) rather than a fail-open default that a misconfigured staff account could inherit. BranchSelectScreen.tsx:43

Several POS endpoints have no branch-scope check

/pos/held-sales (all methods), /pos/quick-items, /pos/customer-search, /pos/upi-vpa, /pos/sales/:id/send-receipt check tenant scope only, not branch. Sessions and sale-creation are properly branch-checked. Confirm whether this is acceptable given upstream gating, or a genuine gap.

Manager overrides granted but unreachable from POS

PRICE_OVERRIDE, PRICE_FLOOR_OVERRIDE, and CREDIT_LIMIT_OVERRIDE exist in the role system and are granted to Sales Manager+, but no POS UI ever sets the corresponding override flags — a price-floor violation hard-blocks a POS sale even for a manager. Confirm whether this is intended (overrides only happen via the main ERP invoice screen) or a missing POS feature.

CASHIER lacks PRICE_LIST_VIEW

Causes a guaranteed, silently-absorbed 403 on one of four parallel reference-data syncs for every plain cashier. See API Reference.

Held sales have no expiry

No TTL column, no cleanup job. They accumulate indefinitely across a terminal's lifetime.

Backend "resume held sale" / "list held sales" endpoints are dead code from the frontend's perspective

GET /pos/held-sales and POST /pos/held-sales/:id/resume exist server-side but are never called by pos-frontend, which resumes purely from its local Dexie copy. Confirm whether this is intentional single-terminal design or an incomplete cross-terminal resume feature.

Unused dependency

zustand is declared in package.json but has zero imports anywhere in src — likely leftover from an earlier state-management approach.

Test coverage gaps (found by absence, not by comment)

No dedicated test covers split-payment math end-to-end, loyalty-point redemption UI, camera barcode scan, or the keyboard shortcuts as a unit — these are only incidentally exercised via the cross-cutting integration test and one Playwright smoke test. Zero TODO/FIXME comments exist anywhere in apps/pos-frontend/src — the codebase uses dated planning-doc references (e.g. OFFLINE-07, PG-051) in comments instead, so there's no in-code breadcrumb of known-incomplete work beyond what's documented here.

Confirmed solid: offline idempotency

The clientOperationId pattern for both sales and customers is genuinely end-to-end, backed by real unique constraints and covered by dedicated tests — not just a UI-layer promise. See Offline Sync Design.

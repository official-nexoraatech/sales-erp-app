Accounting & GST Overview

Seventh module documented from a direct code audit. Covers the chart of accounts, journals, financial reports, period closing, GST returns, e-invoicing, and e-way bills.
Read this before trusting any financial report for a domestic (intrastate) sale

A confirmed bug means that for a regular domestic sale — the ordinary case for most retail transactions — the GST portion is not being posted to the accounting ledger at all. The sale itself records correctly, but the CGST/SGST liability and the tax portion of what the customer owes you are silently left out of every journal, trial balance, and balance sheet. Interstate sales are not affected. Treat your Trial Balance, Profit & Loss, and Balance Sheet as understating your GST position until this is fixed — see Known Issues for the precise mechanism.

Sale returns don't post to accounting or the GST register correctly either

Separately from the above: every approved sale return posts a ₹0 accounting entry (the return's real value never reaches the ledger), and the GST-register recording for a sale return is even more broken — it's likely to fail outright rather than just record a wrong number. If your business processes sale returns, don't rely on the books balancing correctly around them yet.

What's here

  • Chart of Accounts & Journals — your account structure and the underlying double-entry ledger.
  • Financial Reports — Trial Balance, Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow.
  • Financial Period Closing — locking a month/year so nothing more can post into it.
  • GST Returns — GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B data preparation.
  • E-Invoice & E-Way Bill — real government (NIC) integration for B2B invoices.

Chart of Accounts & Journals

Chart of Accounts

Create and manage the accounts your business books post to — assets, liabilities, equity, income, and expense, each with a normal balance side.

Journals

Every posted transaction — a sale, a purchase receipt, a payment — creates a journal automatically behind the scenes. You can browse the journal list to see what's been posted.

The Amount column on the journal list always shows ₹0.00

This is a display bug, not a sign that nothing posted — open the account's own ledger to see the real debit/credit amounts for any transaction.

There is no screen to create a manual journal entry

The journal list has "New Journal" and row-click links, but neither currently leads anywhere — both open a page that doesn't exist. Manual journal entries (the kind an accountant would post directly, outside of a sale/purchase/payment) can only be created by a developer calling the system directly today, not through this screen.

Every journal is still required to balance (debits equal credits) — that rule is enforced by the database itself, not just the screen, so nothing can silently post out of balance.

Financial Reports

Trial Balance, Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow are all available as dedicated pages under Accounting → Reports.

These reports currently understate GST liability on domestic sales

Because of the posting bug described on the Overview page, none of these reports currently reflect the correct GST liability or true receivable amount for intrastate (domestic) sales — only the base sale amount posts. Interstate sales post correctly. Reconcile against your invoice-level GST totals separately until this is fixed; don't treat these reports as the final word on your GST position.

Cash Flow Statement

Splits cash movement into Operating, Investing, and Financing activities.

Every activity row is missing its description

The report correctly calculates amounts, but the description/label text for each line doesn't display — you'll see a blank cell next to each figure. The numbers themselves are unaffected; only the text label is missing.

There's a second "Cash Flow" report elsewhere that gives a different answer

If you run a report literally called "Cash Flow" from the general Reports browser (rather than this dedicated Accounting page), you'll get a simpler calculation with no Investing/Financing breakdown at all — a different, less complete report under a very similar name. See Two Cash Flow Reports That Disagree.

Closing a Financial Period

Locking a month (or closing a full financial year) stops anything new from posting into it — invoices, GRNs, payments, and manual journals dated in a closed period are all rejected consistently across the system.

Year-end close checklist

Before a full year can close, the system runs through a real checklist: no draft invoices left open, no open goods-receipts, all payments allocated, bank reconciliation complete, trial balance genuinely balanced, and a few other integrity checks — then posts real closing entries that zero out income/expense into retained earnings.

The "Owner 2FA confirmed" checklist item doesn't actually check anything itself

One item on the checklist always shows as passed by design — the real two-factor check happens separately, earlier in the process, not as part of this specific checklist step. This isn't a gap in your protection, just worth knowing the checklist item itself is a placeholder for a check that happens elsewhere.

Only Owner-level accounts can close a year or lock a period by default — see Access & Roles.

GST Returns (GSTR-1/3B)

Pick a period and the system assembles your GSTR-1 (B2B, B2C, credit/debit notes, exports, HSN summary) and GSTR-3B data genuinely from your real invoices, purchases, and returns.

"Filing" here means marking it as filed yourself — it does not submit to the government

There is no direct connection from this screen to the GST portal. Preparing the return here gets your numbers ready; you (or your CA) still file the actual return on the government GST portal separately, then come back and mark it "Filed" here for your own record-keeping. Don't assume clicking "Mark as Filed" means the return has actually reached the government — it hasn't.

The "Documents Issued" summary in GSTR-1 is not real data

GSTR-1's required summary of invoice number ranges and cancelled documents is currently a placeholder — it always reports the same fixed starting number and never reports any cancelled invoices, regardless of what actually happened. Because invoice numbers in this system are typed manually rather than issued in a true sequence (see Sales & Invoicing), this section can't yet be computed correctly. Fill in this specific section by hand from your own invoice register before filing — don't submit it as generated.

E-Invoice & E-Way Bill

This one genuinely talks to the government

Unlike GSTR filing, e-invoicing is a real integration with the NIC (government e-invoice) system — when a B2B invoice (one where the customer has a GSTIN) is confirmed, the system automatically requests a real Invoice Reference Number (IRN) and QR code from the government's servers.

For eligible high-value invoices, an e-way bill is generated automatically as a follow-on step once the e-invoice succeeds — you'll see its number and validity on the invoice's E-Invoice screen.

No manual "Generate E-Way Bill" button exists

E-way bills are only ever created automatically as part of the e-invoice flow above — there's no separate screen to trigger one by hand for an invoice that didn't qualify automatically.

Cancelling an e-invoice has a 24-hour window

Past 24 hours from generation, the system can no longer cancel the IRN for you — it flags the invoice for manual cancellation, which you'd need to do directly on the government portal.

Super Admin documentation

Access & Roles

Neither accounting role can open GST returns, and Accountant can't view any financial report

Both dedicated accounting roles have real, significant gaps against what they're named for:

  • Accountant cannot view the Trial Balance, Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, or Cash Flow pages at all, cannot edit or delete an account once created, and — along with Accountant Supervisor — cannot open GSTR-1 or GSTR-3B.
  • Accountant Supervisor can view P&L/Balance Sheet/Trial Balance but not Cash Flow — while the read-only Auditor role can.
  • Only Owner/Super Admin-tier accounts can cancel a generated e-invoice.

If your accounting staff report being unable to open a screen you'd expect them to use daily, this is very likely why — it's a role-configuration gap, not a training issue.

Two Cash Flow Reports That Disagree

Trial Balance, Profit & Loss, and Balance Sheet each exist in two independently-built places in this system: a dedicated Accounting page, and an entry in the general Reports browser. For those three, both versions currently agree with each other.

Cash Flow is the exception — the two versions genuinely disagree

The dedicated Accounting → Cash Flow page correctly splits cash movement into Operating, Investing, and Financing activities. The "Cash Flow" report available from the general Reports browser is a simpler, separately-built calculation that only reports one lump Operating total with no Investing/Financing split at all. Anyone comparing the two will see different structures and different numbers for the same period. Until these are unified, treat the dedicated Accounting → Cash Flow page as the authoritative one.

Limitations to Know

Full technical detail is on Known Issues. Ordered by financial-correctness impact.

  1. GST is not posted to accounting for domestic (intrastate) sales — the single highest-priority item in this module. Every trial balance/P&L/balance sheet since this bug was introduced understates GST liability and receivables for domestic sales.
  2. Sale returns post ₹0 to accounting, and likely fail to record in the GST register at all.
  3. Accountant and Accountant Supervisor roles are missing permissions core to their job — financial-report viewing and GST-return access, respectively/jointly.
  4. GSTR-1's "Documents Issued" section is placeholder data — must be manually corrected before filing.
  5. Two Cash Flow report implementations disagree — see the dedicated page for detail.
  6. No UI exists to post a manual journal entry.

Event-driven posting architecture

Sales/Purchase/Inventory services write domain events to their own outbox table in the same transaction as the business action; accounting-service and gst-service each run Kafka consumers keyed by event type to post journals/GST-ledger entries asynchronously. This is the same outbox pattern already documented in prior modules, applied here for the actual bookkeeping.

Confirmed event payload/consumer mismatches

SALE_RETURN_APPROVED → accounting: wrong field name, posts ₹0

SaleReturnService.ts:236-244 sends totalAmount; SaleReturnAccountingConsumer.ts:9-21 reads p.grandTotal, which is never sent — always evaluates to 0. Confirmed still present (previously flagged in the Sales & Invoicing audit).

SALE_RETURN_APPROVED → GST ledger: near-total payload mismatch, likely throws

SaleReturnGstConsumer.ts:8-27 expects creditNoteNumber, taxableAmount, cgstAmount, sgstAmount, igstAmount, customerGstin, placeOfSupply, hsnCode, gstRate — none of which are ever sent. Since gst_ledger.document_number is NOT NULL, the insert should fail and the consumer's catch block re-throws rather than swallowing the error. gst.ts:77, SaleReturnGstConsumer.ts:76-78

INVOICE_CONFIRMED: interstate flag always miscalculates to true

InvoiceService.ts:520-544 never sends sellerStateCode on the outbox event. InvoiceAccountingConsumer.ts:39 computes isInterstate = p.placeOfSupply !== p.sellerStateCode — since sellerStateCode is always undefined and placeOfSupply is always a real value, this is always true. In PostingMatrixService.buildJournalEntry(), the CGST/SGST branch requires !isInterstate (never true) and the IGST branch requires a nonzero igstAmount (zero for a genuinely intrastate sale) — neither branch ever fires for a domestic sale, so no GST line posts at all; only the base taxable amount posts (DR AR / CR Sales Revenue). The journal still balances (2 equal lines), so nothing errors — this is a silent understatement, not a crash. Interstate invoices are unaffected because their real nonzero igstAmount happens to align with the (wrongly-derived but also-true) flag. InvoiceAccountingConsumer.ts:9-20,39, PostingMatrixService.ts:207-225

GRN and RCM postings are confirmed correct

GRN_APPROVED and RCM_LIABILITY_POSTED event payloads match their consumers field-for-field — an in-code comment documents this was a previously-fixed bug (every ITC/GST line silently zero), and direct comparison confirms the fix holds today.

Data Model

packages/db-client/src/schema/accounting.ts and gst.ts.

accounts / journals / financial_entries

accounts: chart of accounts with accountType (Asset/Liability/Equity/Income/Expense/Contra) and a 19-value accountSubType enum. journals is header-only (no debit/credit totals) — actual double-entry lines live on financial_entries (journalId, accountId, debitAmount, creditAmount, costCenterId).

Journals are balanced at the database level, not just the application level

A deferred constraint trigger re-checks SUM(debit) − SUM(credit) = 0 per journal at transaction commit — an out-of-balance journal cannot be persisted even if application logic were bypassed.

packages/db-client/src/schema/accounting.ts:17-202; migration 0002_phase6_accounting.sql:91-116

financial_years / period_closures

Both carry an independent OPEN/CLOSED status; a period can be locked without closing the whole year. Every posting path (invoice confirm, GRN approve, manual journal, payment) checks the relevant period before posting and throws a period-closed error consistently.

packages/db-client/src/schema/accounting.ts:249-300

gst_ledger / gst_return_filings / gst_2a_entries / einvoice_data

gst_ledger is an append-only register (one row per invoice/credit-note/purchase/purchase-return) with a NOT NULL documentNumber — the exact constraint the sale-return GST consumer bug above violates. gst_return_filings tracks GSTR-1/3B/9/9C status (Pending/Filed/Late Filed/Nil Filed) plus a manual-adjustments field for import-of-goods/services IGST that has no ledger source. einvoice_data combines e-invoice and e-way-bill fields on a single row (IRN, QR code, status, e-way-bill number/validity/status) — there is no separate e-way-bill table.

packages/db-client/src/schema/gst.ts:63-219

Known Issues & Needs Review

Ranked by financial-correctness impact. Everything below is a confirmed code-reading finding unless marked Needs Review. This is the highest-severity module audited so far.

Highest priority: GST never posts to accounting for intrastate (domestic) sales

The interstate/intrastate flag used by the invoice-confirmation accounting consumer is always miscalculated as true because the required sellerStateCode field is never included on the triggering event. As a direct result, neither the CGST/SGST branch nor the IGST branch of the posting logic ever fires for a genuinely intrastate sale — no GST line posts at all, only the base sale amount. This silently understates GST liability and true receivables in every trial balance, P&L, and balance sheet for domestic sales, with zero test coverage catching it. Fully detailed on the API Reference page.

Sale returns: two separate posting bugs on the same event

Accounting posts the return at ₹0 (wrong field name read). The GST-ledger consumer expects roughly ten fields that are never sent and will likely throw outright when gst_ledger's NOT NULL documentNumber constraint is violated by the resulting undefined value.

Accountant/Accountant Supervisor roles are missing core permissions

Accountant cannot view any of the four core financial reports and cannot edit/delete accounts. Neither accounting role can open GSTR-1 or GSTR-3B. Accountant Supervisor specifically lacks Cash Flow view, which the read-only Auditor role does have. No default role has e-invoice cancellation rights.

GSTR-1 "Documents Issued" summary is hardcoded placeholder data

An explicit in-code TODO confirms the invoice-number-range and cancelled-document counts required by this GSTR-1 section are fixed values, not derived from real data — a direct consequence of invoice numbering being free-text rather than a true sequence (see Sales & Invoicing).

Two independently-built Cash Flow report implementations disagree

The dedicated Accounting page's version splits Operating/Investing/Financing correctly; the general Reports browser's version only computes a single lump Operating figure with no breakdown. Trial Balance/P&L/Balance Sheet are aligned between the two engines; Cash Flow is not.

Cash Flow page field-name mismatch

ReportsEngine.getCashFlow() returns a label field per activity row; the frontend reads description, which doesn't exist on the response — every row's description renders blank. Amounts are unaffected.

No UI for manual journal entry

Both the "new journal" and "journal detail" links on the journal list lead to unregistered routes and fall through to a not-found page. Manual journal posting is fully functional server-side but reachable only via direct API call.

Journal list Amount column always shows ₹0.00

Reads a totalDebit field that doesn't exist on the journals table or its API response.

E-way bill has no manual generation UI

The generate endpoint is real and wired into the API client, but no page ever calls it — e-way bills only ever get created automatically as a follow-on to e-invoice generation for qualifying invoices.

GSTR-1/3B "filing" is self-attested, not a real government submission

Worth stating plainly for anyone assuming otherwise: there is no direct call to the GST portal anywhere in this flow. "Mark as Filed" only records that filing happened elsewhere.

Test coverage gap directly explains why the intrastate GST bug went unnoticed

No test exists anywhere for InvoiceAccountingConsumer, SaleReturnAccountingConsumer/SaleReturnGstConsumer, or GRNAccountingConsumer/RcmAccountingConsumer — precisely the consumers where the confirmed bugs above live. Cash-flow investing/financing splits, cost-center P&L, financial-year close checklist, and permission guards are all well tested by contrast.

Confirmed solid: e-invoice/e-way bill NIC integration, and GRN/RCM postings

E-invoice generation is a real, retried HTTP integration against the actual NIC sandbox/production endpoints, not a stub — it fails loudly if not configured rather than faking a response. GRN and reverse-charge (RCM) journal/GST-ledger postings are confirmed correct, including a previously-fixed version of the exact same class of bug found above for sale returns.

Confirmed solid: double-entry integrity and period-closed enforcement

Every journal is balanced at the database level via a deferred constraint trigger, independent of any application-level bug. Period-closed enforcement is consistent across every posting path in the system (invoices, GRNs, manual journals, payments).

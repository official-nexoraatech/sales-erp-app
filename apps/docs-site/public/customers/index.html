Customers & Suppliers Overview

Sixth module documented from a direct code audit. Covers customer records, supplier records, credit limits, loyalty, and customer segments/marketing messages.

This is the master-data module every other part of the app leans on — POS and Sales look up customers here, Purchase looks up suppliers here.

What's here

  • Customers — contact info, billing address, credit terms, loyalty balance, communication preferences.
  • Suppliers — contact info, bank details, credit terms. Simpler than the customer record in a few notable ways — see Managing Suppliers.
  • Segments & Campaigns — group customers by behavior and send SMS/WhatsApp/Email messages.
Two known display/edit issues on customer records

A customer's billing address doesn't display correctly on their detail page, and users with the Sales Manager role may be blocked from opening the customer edit screen even though they can see the Edit button. Neither is something you're doing wrong — see Managing Customers and Access & Roles.

Managing Customers

  1. Customer Details

    Display Name, Customer Type (Retail/Wholesale/B2B/Government/Export), Branch, Phone, Email, GSTIN, PAN.

  2. Billing Address

    Address Line 1, City, State, PIN Code.

  3. Credit Terms

    Credit Limit, Credit Days, Opening Balance.
Billing address may not display correctly

On a customer's detail page, the Address Line 1 and PIN Code fields can show blank even when they were entered — City and State display fine. This is a display-only issue in how that page reads the stored address; the underlying data itself may be fine. If you need to confirm a customer's exact billing address, verify carefully rather than trusting this screen at a glance.

Duplicate phone numbers are allowed

Creating a customer with a phone number that already exists gives a warning, not a block — you can still save it. Worth a quick check before creating a new customer if you're not sure whether they're already in the system.

Communication preferences

Each customer has independent SMS/WhatsApp/Email opt-out checkboxes, honored on both marketing campaigns and automatic birthday greetings — a customer who's opted out of a channel genuinely won't receive messages on it.

Deleting a customer

Deleting deactivates the customer rather than erasing them — their record and history remain, just marked inactive. There's currently no check that stops you from deactivating a customer who still has an outstanding balance, so double-check before doing so if that matters to your bookkeeping.

Merging duplicate customers

Merging two customer records archives the duplicate — it does not currently move that customer's past invoices/payments over to the surviving record. Treat merge as "hide the duplicate," not "combine their full history," for now.

Managing Suppliers

  1. Supplier Details

    Display Name, Supplier Type (Domestic/Import/Manufacturer/Agent), Branch, Phone, Email, GSTIN, PAN.

  2. Bank Details

    Bank Name, IFSC Code, Account Number (masked on screen).

  3. Credit Terms

    Credit Days, Opening Balance.
No address, no credit limit, and no "registered/unregistered" toggle on this form

Three things you might expect aren't here: there's no address section for a supplier at all, no way to set a supplier's credit limit (even though the system can enforce one — see Credit Limits & Loyalty), and no way to mark a supplier as GST-unregistered (which matters for reverse-charge tax handling in Purchase). Every supplier is treated as GST-registered by default, with no way to change that from this screen.

"Stored encrypted" on the account number field

The bank account number field is masked like a password on screen, with a note that it's stored encrypted. Treat this as aspirational rather than a guarantee today — see Data Privacy & Security for the precise, honest picture.

There's no supplier detail/statement page — a supplier's outstanding balance and transaction history currently aren't viewable in the app at all; you'll need to check with your accounts team directly.

Credit Limits & Loyalty

Credit limits

For customers, the Credit Limit set on their record is genuinely enforced — an invoice that would push their balance over the limit is blocked unless the person creating it has override authority. For suppliers, the same enforcement exists in the system, but since there's no field to set a supplier's credit limit anywhere in the app (see Managing Suppliers), it never actually triggers for suppliers set up through the normal screens.

Loyalty points

Where enabled, customers earn points on POS purchases at a fixed rate (₹100 spent = 1 point) and can redeem points at a fixed rate (1 point = ₹0.50). Both rates are the same for every tenant — there's no per-business configuration for them today.

The "loyalty multiplier" on a business season doesn't actually change anything

The Seasons screen lets you set a loyalty multiplier (e.g. "2.0× loyalty" during a festival season) and displays it prominently — but this number currently has no effect on how many points a customer actually earns. Don't rely on it as a real promotional lever yet.

Loyalty points are only earned through POS sales today — a regular (non-POS) invoice doesn't award loyalty points.

Segments & Marketing Messages

There's no separate "customer group" concept — instead, Segments group customers either by a set of ready-made rules (no purchase in 60 days, gold tier, high value, 30+ days overdue, birthdays this month, new this month) or a custom rule you define yourself (by type, status, credit limit, loyalty points, balance, or health score).

Campaigns send an SMS/WhatsApp/Email message to a segment or a specific list of customers. Automatic birthday greetings also go out daily to customers whose birthday falls that day.

Opt-outs are genuinely respected

A customer who's opted out of a channel is excluded from both campaign sends and birthday greetings on that channel — this is real, working protection, not just a checkbox that looks like it does something.

Access & Roles

Sales Manager can see "Edit" on a customer but gets blocked from opening it

The customer edit screen is gated by a different permission than everything else that controls customer editing — the backend save endpoint and the Edit button's visibility both use one permission, but the page route itself checks a different one. No default role except Owner/Admin holds that second permission, so a Sales Manager (who legitimately has edit rights everywhere else) will see the Edit button, click it, and land on an Access Denied screen. The equivalent Supplier edit route does not have this bug. This needs a role-configuration or route fix — it isn't something a Sales Manager can work around on their own.

ActionPermission checkedNotes
View / create customerCUSTOMER_VIEW / CUSTOMER_CREATECashier has both
Edit customer (save)CUSTOMER_EDITSales Manager has this
Open customer edit screenCUSTOMER_UPDATEOnly Owner/Admin have this — the mismatch above
Delete / merge customerCUSTOMER_DELETE / CUSTOMER_MERGE
View / create / edit supplierSUPPLIER_VIEW/CREATE/EDITconsistent — no mismatch here
Several defined permissions are never actually checked anywhere

CUSTOMER_BLOCK, dedicated credit-limit-view/update permissions, a dedicated statement-view permission, and supplier equivalents for update/bank-view/import/export all exist as concepts in the permission system but aren't enforced by any real screen or endpoint. Don't rely on granting or withholding these to control access — the coarser CUSTOMER_EDIT/SUPPLIER_EDIT-style permissions are what actually gate things.

Data Privacy & Security

Customer GSTIN/PAN and supplier bank details are not actually encrypted, despite what the code comments and UI say

A customer's GSTIN and PAN, and a supplier's bank account number, are currently stored as plain, readable text in the database — only a one-way hash is kept alongside them to support search. The database schema and the supplier form's own helper text both describe these fields as encrypted; that description does not match what the code actually does today. If encryption of this data is a compliance requirement for your business, treat it as not yet implemented for customers/suppliers specifically.

By contrast, the HR & Payroll side of this system does genuinely encrypt equivalent fields (employee PAN, bank details, salary figures) using a real encryption utility — so the capability exists in the codebase, it just hasn't been applied here yet. This is a targeted, closeable gap, not a fundamental limitation.

Be cautious of one existing automated test in this area

There's a test that appears to verify GSTIN is stored encrypted, but it does so by encrypting the value itself before inserting it directly into the database — bypassing the real customer-creation process entirely. It doesn't prove the actual create screen encrypts anything, and shouldn't be read as evidence that this gap is already covered.

Limitations to Know

Full technical detail is on Known Issues.

  1. Customer GSTIN/PAN and supplier bank details aren't encrypted, despite documentation and UI text implying otherwise — a real compliance gap if that matters to you.
  2. Sales Manager is blocked from the customer edit screen by a route-permission mismatch.
  3. Supplier records have no address, credit limit, or GST-registration-status fields — several supplier-side capabilities that exist in the backend are unreachable through the UI.
  4. No supplier statement or outstanding-balance view exists anywhere in the app, despite a working calculation existing internally.
  5. Customers/suppliers can be deactivated with an outstanding balance — no warning or block today.
  6. Merging customers doesn't move their transaction history to the surviving record.

Customer form: a Needs-Review data-flow concern

Address fields may not actually reach the save payload

CustomerFormPage.tsx registers billing-address inputs as billingAddress.addressLine1 etc., then tries to read them back off the submitted data as flat, literally-dotted keys (d['billingAddress.addressLine1']). React Hook Form's default behavior treats a dot in a field name as a nested path, not a literal key — if that's what's happening here, the flat-key reads would always be undefined, meaning the address the user types would never be included in the create/edit request at all. This wasn't confirmed by running the app, so it's flagged as Needs Review rather than a certain bug — but it's worth a runtime check before assuming customer addresses save correctly. CustomerFormPage.tsx:87-104,151-161

Confirmed separately: the address display bug on CustomerViewPage

Regardless of the above, CustomerViewPage.tsx reads billing.addressLine1/billing.pinCode, but the actual stored object uses line1/pincode — plain property-name mismatch, confirmed by comparing the two files directly. Only City and State (whose names happen to match) will ever render. CustomerViewPage.tsx:216-221, customer.routes.ts:27-37, master.ts:78-86

Known Issues & Needs Review

Ranked by real-world impact. Everything below is a confirmed code-reading finding unless marked Needs Review.

Customer PII and supplier bank details are not actually encrypted

Both schemas and the supplier form's own copy describe GSTIN/PAN/bank-account fields as encrypted, but the write path (customer.routes.ts:288, comment: "simplified: store plaintext here") persists plaintext with only a SHA-256 hash for search. Real field encryption (encryptField/decryptField from @erp/utils/server) already exists and is used for HR/payroll data elsewhere in the codebase — it simply isn't applied here. A misleading integration test encrypts the value manually before a direct DB insert, bypassing the real endpoint, and shouldn't be read as coverage of this gap.

customers/:id/edit route/permission mismatch

CUSTOMER_UPDATE gates the route; CUSTOMER_EDIT gates the button and the actual save endpoint. Only Owner/Admin hold CUSTOMER_UPDATE by default — Sales Manager (who has CUSTOMER_EDIT) sees the button and is denied on click. Supplier's equivalent route has no such mismatch.

Customer address may never reach the save payload (Needs Review — not runtime-confirmed)

CustomerFormPage.tsx registers address fields with dotted names and reads them back as literal flat keys — a mismatch with React Hook Form's default nested-path behavior for dotted field names. If confirmed at runtime, this would mean a customer's typed address is silently dropped on every create/edit. Confirmed separately and independently: CustomerViewPage.tsx reads the wrong property names (addressLine1/pinCode vs the actual line1/pincode) when displaying a saved address — that part is a certain bug regardless of the form question.

Supplier credit limit and RCM registration status are enforced but unreachable via UI

PurchaseOrderService checks suppliers.creditLimit/creditLimitEnabled; GRNService checks suppliers.isRegistered for reverse-charge tax. Neither field has a form control or a Zod schema field anywhere in the supplier create/edit path — both are permanently stuck at their DB defaults (credit limit disabled, registered=true) for any supplier created through the standard UI.

Loyalty multiplier per season has no effect

business_seasons.loyaltyMultiplier is fully editable and displayed in the Seasons UI but is never read by LoyaltyService's earn/redeem logic.

No outstanding-balance check on customer/supplier deactivation

Customer delete has an explicit, unimplemented TODO acknowledging this gap. Supplier delete has the identical gap with no acknowledging comment at all.

Customer merge doesn't re-point transactions

Only the source record is archived; its own code comment describes re-pointing transactions as a "Phase 5+" item, not yet done.

Three redundant, all-unused statement/outstanding implementations

One stub for customers, one stub plus one real implementation for suppliers (in a different service) — none called from any frontend page.

Dead permission constants

CUSTOMER_BLOCK, dedicated credit-limit and statement-view permissions, SUPPLIER_UPDATE, SUPPLIER_BANK_VIEW, SUPPLIER_IMPORT/EXPORT are all defined but never checked by any route or guard. customers.status = 'BLOCKED' is schema-valid but no code path ever sets it.

Test coverage gap

No supplier integration test exists at all. No form-submission test exists for either CustomerFormPage or SupplierFormPage — meaning the address-field concern above is untested either way.

Confirmed fixed since an earlier internal note: opt-out enforcement

An older note claimed opt-out flags weren't enforced on campaign sends or birthday greetings. Current code enforces both correctly — CampaignService filters recipients by the matching opt-out column before every send, and the birthday-greeting job checks per-channel opt-out before firing. If you're consulting older internal audit notes, treat this specific finding as resolved.

Confirmed fixed since an earlier internal note: user.branchIds at runtime

An older note claimed a user's branch scope was always empty at runtime despite being present in the JWT. Current login code populates it correctly from a dedicated endpoint, and both the customer and supplier forms read and auto-apply it correctly for single-branch users.

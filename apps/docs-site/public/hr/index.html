Client documentation

HR & Payroll Overview

Eighth module documented from a direct code audit. Covers employees, attendance/leave, payroll, statutory filings, Form 16, and alteration orders (tailoring).
HR Manager is currently locked out of Alteration Orders entirely

Alterations sits directly under the HR menu, but the HR Manager role doesn't have permission to open, create, or update a single alteration order — despite this being exactly the kind of day-to-day task this role exists for. If your HR staff report they can't do anything on the Alterations screen, this is why. See Access & Roles.

Several things your printed HR training material describes don't actually exist yet

Full & final settlement on exit, leave encashment, manual leave-balance adjustment, a bank-transfer export file, "sync biometric device" button, and bulk email/WhatsApp/ZIP delivery of salary slips are all described in HR training material but are not implemented — see the relevant pages below and Known Issues for the exact list.

What's here

  • Employees — master records, with genuinely encrypted PAN and bank account fields.
  • Attendance & Leave — daily marking, leave application/approval.
  • Payroll — a real PF/ESI/professional-tax/TDS calculation engine, run monthly.
  • Statutory Filings & Form 16 — PF/ESI challan prep, annual tax certificate data.
  • Alteration Orders — tailoring/stitching job tracking.

Managing Employees

The employee form has three tabs: Basic (name, phone, gender, date of birth, last 4 digits of Aadhaar only), Employment (type, joining date, department, designation), and Bank & Tax (PAN, bank details, UAN, ESI number, PF/ESI applicability).

PAN and bank account number are genuinely encrypted

Unlike the customer/supplier records elsewhere in this ERP (where "encrypted" fields turned out to still be plaintext), an employee's PAN and bank account number really are encrypted at rest here — the label on the form is accurate.

An employee's photo and documents (Aadhaar, PAN, certificates, offer letter) can be uploaded and downloaded from their profile, along with a leave-balance summary and — if you have salary-viewing rights — whether salary data exists for them.

When someone leaves

"Record Exit" captures an exit date and reason and marks the employee inactive.

No full-and-final settlement calculation happens automatically

Exiting an employee doesn't calculate anything — no pending-salary total, no leave encashment, no advance reconciliation, no settlement document. If your process depends on the system producing an F&F settlement figure, that has to be worked out manually today.

There's no employee-delete option — only "exit," which is the correct/expected behavior for maintaining historical payroll records.

Attendance & Leave

Attendance

Mark attendance individually or in bulk, or import from a biometric device's CSV export.

There's no live "sync biometric device" button

Attendance import only works from a CSV file today — there's no direct, one-click sync with a biometric machine. If your training material describes a sync button, that feature isn't built yet.

Leave

Apply for leave, and (if you have approval rights) approve or reject pending requests. Approving deducts from the employee's leave balance and marks those days present-on-leave for payroll purposes.

Rejecting leave doesn't let you type a reason

A rejected leave request is always recorded with the same generic reason — there's no field to explain why.

If an employee has no leave-balance record yet, their leave request isn't checked against any limit

The balance check only runs if a balance record already exists for that employee and leave type. A newly added employee without an initialized balance can have a leave request approved with no cap at all. Make sure leave balances are set up for every employee before relying on this check.

There's no way to manually adjust a leave balance (e.g. a goodwill extra day) or encash unused leave — both would need to happen outside the system today.

Running Payroll

1
Create the run for a period, Calculate
→
2
Approve (often a different, more senior person)
→
3
Disburse — permanent, cannot be undone
By design, the person who calculates payroll usually isn't the one who approves it

HR staff can create and calculate a payroll run, but approving and disbursing it is reserved for an Owner/Admin-level account in most setups — a deliberate separation of duties, not a bug. If your HR Manager can't find an Approve button, that's expected.

Genuinely calculated per employee: PF (employee + employer share), ESI (for lower earners), professional tax (correctly varies by the employee's state), TDS (income-tax withholding), and any active loan EMI deduction — all based on real attendance and leave data for the period.

TDS tax slabs may be out of date

The income-tax withholding calculation uses a fixed set of slab rates tied to a specific past financial year, with no setting to update them for a new year. Have your accountant verify the TDS figures against the current year's actual slabs before relying on them, especially at the start of a new financial year.

Once approved, a payroll run cannot be changed for that month

This is intentional — recalculating is blocked the moment a run is approved, and there's no "undo" once disbursed. Double-check a run carefully before approving it.

Employee loans

Employee loans have no screen anywhere in the app

The system can track an employee loan and automatically deduct its EMI from payroll each month, correctly post it to accounting, and stop once repaid — all of that works. But there is currently no page anywhere to create, view, or manage a loan. This is a fully backend-only feature today; using it requires a developer to call the system directly.

Payslips

View a slip on screen and print it via your browser. There's no bulk "email/WhatsApp all payslips" or "download all as ZIP" action — even though this is described in HR training material, it isn't wired up end-to-end yet.

Statutory Filings & Form 16

PF and ESI challan screens show per-employee contribution totals for a period, let you export a CSV for the government portal, and let you mark the challan as filed for your own records (this doesn't file anything with the government automatically).

Form 16

Pick an employee and financial year to generate a summary of their annual gross pay and tax withheld.

Form 16 downloads as raw data, not a certificate

"Download" produces a plain data file, not a formatted PDF certificate, and there's no official certificate number attached to it. Treat this as the underlying figures to build your official Form 16 from, not a document you can hand to an employee as-is.

Alteration Orders

HR Manager accounts currently can't use this screen at all

See Access & Roles — this needs a role-configuration fix, not anything you're doing wrong.

The real status flow

An alteration order actually moves through seven stages: Received → Assigned → In Progress → Quality Check → Ready → Delivered (Cancelled is possible from any point before Delivered). If your training material describes a simpler four-step flow (Received/In Progress/Ready/Delivered), the real system has two extra steps — Assigned (to a specific tailor) and Quality Check.

No token slip is printed

Creating an order doesn't produce a printable token slip — there's no print/receipt output for alteration orders at all yet. Orders are also found by their order number, not a separate "token number" — those are the same thing in this system.

Delivering an order requires it to be in "Ready" status and the payment collected to cover the full balance due.

Access & Roles

Intentional: payroll approval is separated from payroll processing

HR Manager can create and calculate a payroll run, but cannot approve or disburse it, cannot bulk-send payslips, and cannot print the PDF payslip — those require an Owner/Admin-level account by default. This matches a deliberate segregation-of-duties pattern reflected elsewhere in the system's workflow configuration, not an oversight.

Unintentional-looking gaps: HR Manager is missing several permissions core to its own job
  • Alteration orders — cannot view, create, or update any alteration order, despite Alterations sitting directly under the HR menu this role uses.
  • Holiday calendar — cannot manage it, despite HR training material instructing this role to do exactly that.
  • Monthly attendance reports — cannot view them, despite being instructed to check this before every payroll run.
  • Rejecting leave — can approve leave but not reject it.
  • Applying for its own leave — cannot submit a leave application for itself.

None of these look like deliberate restrictions the way payroll-approval separation does — they read as role-configuration gaps worth fixing.

Regular staff can see the "Apply for Leave" form even without permission to submit it

The leave screen shows the application form to everyone, but a Staff-level account that lacks leave-application rights will be rejected only after clicking Submit — worth knowing if staff report a confusing error at that point.

Data Privacy & Encryption

This module's encryption claims are genuinely accurate — a contrast worth noting

Employee PAN, bank account number, and full salary-structure figures (CTC, basic, HRA, DA, gross) are all really encrypted at rest. This is the one place in the system so far where "encrypted" in the UI copy matches what the code actually does — unlike customer/supplier records elsewhere.

Coverage is inconsistent within this module itself

A generated payslip only encrypts its gross and net totals — the individual PF, ESI, professional tax, TDS, and loan-deduction line amounts on that same payslip are stored as plain, readable figures. Employee loan balances and EMI amounts aren't encrypted at all either, despite being just as salary-sensitive as the figures that are protected. If full protection of salary-adjacent data matters for your compliance posture, this is a gap worth closing.

Limitations to Know

Full technical detail is on Known Issues.

  1. HR Manager is locked out of Alteration Orders entirely — likely your highest-priority role-configuration fix.
  2. TDS slabs are fixed to a past financial year with no way to update them — verify payroll tax figures manually each new financial year until this is addressed.
  3. Employee loans have no UI — fully backend-only today.
  4. Bulk payslip delivery (email/WhatsApp/ZIP) doesn't work end-to-end, despite being documented.
  5. Full & final settlement, leave encashment, and manual leave adjustment don't exist — all manual processes today.
  6. Payslip line items and employee loan figures aren't encrypted, despite being as sensitive as the salary-structure fields that are.

Architecture

Frontend: apps/web-frontend/src/pages/hr/. Backend: apps/hr-service.

Routes

All under the nav's HR group: Employees, Attendance, Leave, Payroll, PF Challan, ESI Challan, Form 16, Alterations. hr/holidays is registered but absent from the nav menu — reachable only by direct URL.

apps/web-frontend/src/App.tsx:342-357, apps/web-frontend/src/lib/navigation.ts:168-182

Employee Loans: complete backend, zero frontend

No page, no API-client entry, no nav link

apps/hr-service/src/api/employee-loans.routes.ts, EmployeeLoanService.ts, the DB tables, and even the accounting-event integration (EMPLOYEE_LOAN_DISBURSED → EmployeeLoanAccountingConsumer.ts) are all fully implemented and tested. Confirmed by exhaustive grep of apps/web-frontend/src for "loan" — zero hits outside this audit.

Alteration events: published to a bus nothing listens to, notifications sent a different way

Inconsistent integration pattern

ALTERATION_RECEIVED/ALTERATION_DELIVERED/ALTERATION_STATUS_CHANGED are published to the outbox event bus but have zero consumers anywhere in the codebase. Customer notifications for assignment/ready-for-pickup are instead sent via a direct, synchronous HTTP call straight into notification-service, bypassing the event bus entirely. Both mechanisms currently coexist for the same feature area — not broken, but worth normalizing to one pattern. apps/hr-service/src/api/alteration.routes.ts:62-81,142,204-209,236-246,248,279

An unclear workflow-seed layer

A system "workflow" seed defines approval templates keyed on strings like PAYROLL_PROCESS/EMPLOYEE_CREATE/LEAVE_APPLY — but these match permission-constant names, not the actual event names hr-service publishes (EMPLOYEE_JOINED/LEAVE_APPLIED). No dedicated workflow-service exists; whether/how this layer is actually triggered off live events wasn't fully traceable in this pass — flagged as Needs Review rather than asserted broken. packages/platform-sdk/src/workflow.ts:115-122,158-173

Payroll calculation constants

PF: employee 12% of basic (capped at ₹15,000 basic), employer split 8.33% EPS (capped ₹1,250/month) + remainder EPF — hardcoded, not per-tenant configurable. ESI: 0.75%/3.25% employee/employer, only if gross ≤ ₹21,000 — hardcoded. Professional tax: genuinely resolved per-state from a seeded slab table keyed by state code, with free-text branch/organization state names normalized to codes — this part is not hardcoded and correctly handles India's multi-state PT variation. TDS: Section-192-style monthly withholding, standard deduction ₹75,000, slabs at 5/10/15/20/30% — explicitly commented as "FY 2024-25" slabs with no effective-dated versioning, unlike the PT slab table.

Data Model

packages/db-client/src/schema/hr.ts.

employees

aadhaarLast4 (4 chars only, by design), panEncrypted+panHash, bankAccountNoEncrypted+bankAccountNoHash — genuinely encrypted, HMAC-SHA256 hash columns for exact-match lookup only (not unique constraints — duplicate PAN across employees isn't prevented at the DB level). bankName/bankIfsc remain plaintext.

employee_salaries / payroll_runs / payroll_slips

employee_salaries: ctc/basic/hra/da/allowances/gross all encrypted, with an explicit in-code comment: never cache, never log, never return from a list endpoint. payroll_runs: status DRAFT→CALCULATING→CALCULATED→APPROVED→DISBURSED, unique per (tenant, month, year); carries salaryJournalId/disbursalJournalId columns that no route ever populates — dead columns, since the real journal linkage lives entirely on the accounting-service side via events. payroll_slips: only grossSalary/netSalary are encrypted (ciphertext-bearing text columns) — PF/ESI/PT/TDS/loan-deduction/basic/HRA/DA/allowance amounts are all plain decimals. No slip/certificate number column exists; the numeric row id is the only identifier.

alteration_orders / alteration_tasks

alteration_orders.status: 7-value enum RECEIVED/ASSIGNED/IN_PROGRESS/QUALITY_CHECK/READY/DELIVERED/CANCELLED, unique orderNumber generated post-insert. alteration_tasks is defined in the schema but never read or written by any route — dead table, superseded by the simpler assigned-tailor-plus-status-on-order model actually in use.

Other tables

pt_slabs is deliberately global/non-tenant-scoped reference data keyed by state code and effective date range — states with no PT law intentionally have zero rows. employee_loans/loan_deduction_history: flat, no-interest EMI with a full per-slip deduction audit trail — not encrypted, despite being salary-adjacent.

Known Issues & Needs Review

Ranked by real-world impact. Everything below is a confirmed code-reading finding unless marked Needs Review.

HR Manager cannot use Alteration Orders, Holidays, or Attendance Reports

None of ALTERATION_VIEW/CREATE/UPDATE, HR_MANAGE, or ATTENDANCE_REPORT are granted to the HR Manager role by default, despite the role's own training material instructing exactly these actions. Unlike the payroll-approval gap (which has clear segregation-of-duties justification elsewhere in the codebase), nothing suggests these are intentional.

Employee Loans: fully implemented backend, zero frontend

Routes, service logic, database tables, tests, and even correct accounting-event integration all exist and work. No page, API-client method, or nav link exists anywhere in apps/web-frontend.

Bulk payslip delivery doesn't work end-to-end

POST /payroll-runs/:id/bulk-send is real and publishes a SALARY_SLIP_READY event, but that event has zero consumers anywhere in the codebase, no email/WhatsApp actually goes out, no ZIP download exists, and the frontend never even calls this endpoint. The feature described in HR training material doesn't exist end-to-end at any layer beyond the single publish call.

TDS slabs are hardcoded to a specific past financial year with no update mechanism

Unlike the professional-tax slab table (which is genuinely effective-dated per state), income-tax withholding uses fixed constants explicitly commented as "FY 2024-25" with no versioning table — these will silently go stale every financial year without a code change.

Payslip and loan figures are inconsistently encrypted

payroll_slips only encrypts gross/net totals; every individual statutory-deduction and loan-deduction amount on the same row is plaintext. employee_loans isn't encrypted at all. Contrast with employee_salaries, which correctly encrypts every compensation figure.

Several HR-training-documented features don't exist in code at all

Full & final settlement calculation on exit, leave encashment, manual leave-balance adjustment, a bank-transfer export file, and a "sync biometric device" button are all described in training material but absent from both frontend and backend — confirmed by targeted grep, not just difficulty finding them. The nightly biometric-sync scheduler job is a literal no-op stub that always reports zero imported records.

Alteration orders differ from their own training description

The real flow has seven states (adding Assigned and Quality Check) versus the four-state flow described in cashier training material; no token-slip printing exists at all; and orders are found by order number, not a separate "token number" concept.

Inconsistent event architecture for alterations

Alteration lifecycle events are published to the outbox bus with zero consumers, while the actual customer notifications for the same lifecycle moments are sent via a separate, direct synchronous HTTP call. Both mechanisms exist for the same feature; only one does anything.

Leave balance check is skippable

If no balance record exists yet for an employee/leave-type combination, the balance check is bypassed entirely rather than defaulting to zero-allowed — a newly onboarded employee could have unlimited leave approved until their balance is explicitly initialized.

Dead/orphaned schema and permissions

payroll_runs.salaryJournalId/disbursalJournalId columns are never written by any route. alteration_tasks table is never read or written by any route. The SALARY_VIEW permission constant (granted to HR Manager) is never checked by any route guard anywhere.

A regular Staff account can submit a leave application it doesn't have permission for

The "Apply for Leave" panel isn't gated on the submit permission client-side — a Staff user without leave-apply rights sees the form and only gets rejected at submit time, a confusing UX rather than a security issue (the backend correctly blocks it).

Confirmed solid: payroll's own accounting-event wiring

Unlike the equivalent invoice/sale-return event postings audited in Accounting & GST, payroll-run approval/disbursal and employee-loan-disbursal events are published and consumed with matching field names and correct posting-matrix entries — no mismatch found here.

Confirmed solid: professional tax multi-state handling, and the core payroll/statutory test suite

PT is genuinely computed per-state via seeded, effective-dated reference data — not hardcoded to one state. PF/ESI/PT/TDS math, Form-16 aggregation, and loan-EMI computation are all covered by real tests that exercise actual domain logic, not mocked-away assertions.

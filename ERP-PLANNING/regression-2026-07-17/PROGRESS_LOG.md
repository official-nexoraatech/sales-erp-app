## Flagged, NOT fixed — needs a payroll/tax domain decision, not a unilateral code change

- **Payroll TDS deducted on a ₹0-gross payslip (zero attendance/full LOP), Net Salary silently clamped to ₹0 instead of showing the true negative figure.** Traced root cause: `PayrollEngine.ts:289` computes `tdsDeduction = computeMonthlyTDS(grossFull * 12)` — off the employee's full _nominal_ annual salary, not the LOP-adjusted actual gross for the period. This is standard, defensible payroll design (annualized-projection withholding, trued up over the year) — **not itself a bug**. The actual bug is `PayrollEngine.ts:291`: `netSalary = Math.max(0, grossSalary - totalDeductions)` silently clamps a legitimately negative net pay to ₹0, hiding that this employee's deductions exceeded their period earnings rather than surfacing it. Not fixed here because the _correct_ business rule (show the true negative? cap deductions at gross and carry forward the shortfall? something else?) is a payroll/tax policy decision, not something to invent unilaterally in a QA pass — flagging for the user/a payroll-domain owner to decide, then it's a one-line fix once the rule is chosen.
